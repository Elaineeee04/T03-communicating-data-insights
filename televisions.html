Buying a New TV? Let’s Explore Energy Efficiency

Buying a new TV? Screen size, brand, and features matter — but energy consumption affects your electricity bills every day.

This page helps consumers understand TV options, sizes, brands, and energy use so they can make smarter choices.

Let’s begin by looking at the types of TVs available in Australia.

What types of screen technologies are available?

Most models in Australia are LCD (LED), while OLEDs and LCDs are less frequent.

Once you know the types, the next step is choosing the right size and brand.

What screen sizes are most common?

A wide range of TV screen sizes are currently available, from smaller models such as 16–20 inches up to larger sizes like 100–116 inches. The most frequent sizes are 55”, 65”, and 75”.

After deciding on size, the next key factor is the brand.

Which brands have the largest number of models?

Different brands offer a range of models with varying screen technologies and energy ratings. Some brands, like LG, Samsung, and Kogan, provide a wider selection of energy-efficient models, giving consumers more choices.

Now that we’ve explored what’s available, it’s time to see how these features translate into everyday energy use.

Which technologies are actually the most energy-efficient?

The screen technology that consumes the least amount of power is LCD, making it the most energy-efficient choice.

Of course, TV size also matters — bigger screens usually use more energy. Let’s check.

How does screen size affect power use?

As the screen size increases, the average power consumption generally increases. Bigger TVs = bigger bills.

So what does this mean for consumers? Let’s summarise.

Recommendations for TV Buyers

  • Choose LCD (LED) if energy efficiency is your main concern.
  • Smaller screens mean lower power bills.
  • Popular brands offer variety, but focus on screen size and technology to save energy and money.

Choose the right TV for your needs: balance size, features, and long-term energy savings.
